# Wove Brand Voice — Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-06-13
**Owner:** Luca
**Applies to:** All ad captions, in-product copy, marketing site, social posts, founder voiceover scripts (with stated exceptions).
**Status:** Approved — first application: re-caption pass on Ads 1A, 1B, 2A.

---

## 1. Purpose

Wove needs a single, internalized voice that holds across every surface — paid ads, the app's own toasts, the marketing site, founder content — so each piece of copy reads like it belongs to the same brand. The trigger for this spec was a single caption ("Now we get you.") that read as smug. The root cause was deeper: the system was speaking from a corporate "we" register, defaulting to generic phrasing, and treating every caption beat at the same volume. This spec replaces that with explicit rules.

## 2. Audience

**Eclectic — anyone who cares about clothes and resale.** The voice has to read as cool to a 19-year-old in Brooklyn who follows fitcheck accounts AND a 32-year-old in Austin who buys vintage on The RealReal. That rules out heavy Gen-Z slang (alienates the older end) and rules out polished millennial gloss (alienates the younger end). The voice lives in the overlap: confident, specific, opinion-having, but not insider.

## 3. Voice anchors

**SSENSE × Liquid Death, balanced 50/50.**

- **SSENSE side:** Confident through specificity. Names eras, regions, garments, prices. Trusts the reader. Lets the photo carry meaning. Underexplains.
- **Liquid Death side:** Quotable, opinion-having, occasionally sharp. Punches up at boring incumbents (fast fashion, scam DMs, Depop drama, $40 shipping). Built to be screenshot-forwarded.
- **Blend dial:** Hooks pull toward Liquid Death. Mid-ad reveals pull toward SSENSE. UI register stays functional. Celebration register stays terse.

## 4. Personality pillars

Three pillars. Every caption has to pass all three.

1. **Specific over general.** Drop names, eras, regions, gestures, prices. "Y2K mesh. East London. $24." reads. "Vintage clothes you'll love." doesn't.
2. **Trust the reader.** Don't explain the joke. Don't explain the product. The visual carries the meaning; the caption sharpens it.
3. **Punch up at boring, not at the user.** The voice has stakes — it's against fast-fashion gloss, scam DMs, $40 shipping, Depop drama. Never at the audience's intelligence.

## 5. Self-reference rule

**Wove refers to itself in third person, or not at all. Never "we."**

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do |
|---|---|
| "We learn what you like." | "It clocks you in 5 swipes." |
| "We take 10%." | "Wove takes 10." |
| "Now we get you." | "Calibrated to your taste." |

Reason: "we" reads as a boardroom addressing the user. Wove-in-third-person makes Wove a thing in the world — the user is seeing how the product behaves, not being lectured at.

**Exception:** Founder-persona ads (future batches when Luca is on camera). First-person is fine — that's a real person talking, not the brand.

## 6. Structural rules

| Rule | Direction |
|---|---|
| **Hook length** | 1–6 words. Less is more. |
| **Mid-ad length** | ≤12 words. |
| **Casing** | Sentence case default. `ALL CAPS` reserved for celebration toasts ("ORDER PLACED") and brand-frame moments. No all-lowercase — reads try-hard to the older audience half. |
| **Punctuation** | Periods over commas. Em-dashes welcome. **No exclamation marks, ever.** No ellipses. |
| **Emoji** | Max one per caption, and only when it IS the thing (🪙 for Coin Flip ✓). No decorative emoji. Strike ❤️ on heart UIs — redundant. |
| **Pronouns** | "You" sparingly — only when the line lands harder for it. **No "we."** Default to no pronoun. |

## 7. Vocabulary

### Use freely
- **Specific anchors** — brand names, eras (Y2K, 70s, 90s), garment language (drape, hand, fit, silhouette), prices, sizes, conditions
- **Resale-native verbs** — *flip, drop, score, find, list, save*
- **Product terms** — *swipe, tap, save, send a flip*
- **Energy descriptors** — *clean, sharp, calibrated, dialed, locked in, the move*
- **Anti-incumbent jabs** — *dropship, $40 shipping, drama, scams, mall brands*

### Never use
- **Startup slop** — *revolutionizing, redefining, empowering, journey, ecosystem, reimagining, seamless, curated, members*
- **Dated try-hard slang** — *slay, slaps, bussin', no cap, fire, low-key, fr fr*
- **Hype-bro** — *insane, crazy, mind-blowing, literally, obsessed*
- **Soft startup-speak** — *hello, welcome, discover, dream, journey, inspired*
- **2022 peak-internet** — *vibe, iconic, main character, era* (as in "my X era")
- **AI/tech reveals** — *algorithm, AI, machine learning, recommendation engine* (use the actual feature name: Style DNA, Coin Flip)

### Use sparingly (once each across the entire ad set)
- *Mid* — clean diss, ages fast.
- *Cop* — borderline slang, only if it lands cleanly in context.
- *Drop* — fine for actual drops; not as filler.

## 8. Per-beat tone matrix

The voice doesn't sit on one setting. Each ad beat gets a different dial position.

| Beat | Tone dial | Why | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook** (0–1.5s) | Lean **Liquid Death** — punchy, quotable, opinion-having | First 1.5s decides whether it gets forwarded | "Resale, but you have fun." / "5 swipes. Calibrated." |
| **Mid-ad reveal** (2–10s) | Lean **SSENSE** — specific, terse, descriptive | The visual is doing the work; sharpen, don't editorialize | "It clocks your taste." / "Flip the seller. Win 50% off." |
| **UI toasts** | Functional. System voice. Lowercase friendly here as a *system* register. | These are app-message, not brand-message | `Saved to Liked` ✓ |
| **Celebration toast** | ALL CAPS, single phrase, no exclamation. | Maximum signal, zero friction | `ORDER PLACED` ✓ |
| **CTA outro** | **Brand voice OFF.** Pragmatic. Restraint. | Action matters more than cleverness | `Join the waitlist · wove.shop/waitlist` stays |

**The mistake to avoid:** cranking every beat to "hook" level. The hook earns its energy because the rest of the ad stays cool around it.

## 9. Audit of shipped ads (Batch 1)

8 captions need rewrites. 11 are already on-brand. Hit rate of the prior system: ~65%.

| # | Ad | Old | New | Rule applied |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1A hook | "Swipe. Match. Buy." | **"Resale, but you have fun."** | Specific > general; punch at boring incumbents; "match" was conceptually wrong anyway. |
| 2 | 1A mid 1 | "The app learns your taste." | **"5 swipes. Calibrated."** | Specific over general. Drop the gerund. |
| 3 | 1A mid 2 | "Send a Coin Flip 🪙" | **"Flip the seller for 50% off."** | "Send a" was meek; name the stake. |
| 4 | 1A PayoutChip sub | "We take 10%." | **"Wove takes 10."** | No "we." Drop the % on subline (headline already says "Sellers keep 90%"). |
| 5 | 1B mid | "Tap. Pay. Done." | **"One tap. Shipped."** | Specific over general; "shipped" is the actual outcome. |
| 6 | 1B order sub | "Vintage Denim Stack · shipping to you" | **"Vintage Levi's. Shipping tomorrow."** | Name the brand; name the when. |
| 7 | 2A hook | "We learn what you like." | **"It clocks you in 5 swipes."** | No "we." Edge word ("clocks") + specific number. |
| 8 | 2A reveal | "Now we get you." | **"Calibrated to your taste."** | No "we." Loses the smug, keeps the payoff. |

### Kept as-is (already on brand)
- Ad 1A: "YOU WIN", "OFFER ACCEPTED", "$34 $68" strikethrough, all celebration register
- Ad 1B: "Tinder, but for thrift." (the hook nails Balanced), "Saved to Liked" toast, "ORDER PLACED"
- Ad 2A: TasteTags ("VINTAGE", "EARTH", "UTILITY"), "94% match" badge
- All ads: CTA outro

## 10. Future-ad checklist (use this before any new caption ships)

Before approving any new caption, it must pass all of these:

1. **No "we."** Wove in third person or not at all.
2. **Specific or punchy** — never both generic and soft.
3. **Pillar test** — passes all three pillars (specific, trust reader, punch at boring not user).
4. **Casing + punctuation** — sentence case unless celebration; no `!`.
5. **Vocabulary** — no terms from the "never use" list.
6. **Beat dial** — tone matches the beat type (hook = sharper, mid = specific, UI = functional, CTA = pragmatic).
7. **Read it out loud** — if it sounds like a startup landing page, it fails.

## 11. Open questions

- **Lowercase exceptions:** If the founder's persona content (later batches) leans into a more casual register, we may need a per-persona override. Not blocking — flag when we get there.
- **Multi-language:** Spec is English-only. If Wove ships to non-EN markets, voice translation will need its own pass.
- **Long-form contexts:** This spec is tuned for short-form ad captions (≤12 words). Email subjects, landing-page paragraphs, and onboarding copy may need a secondary spec.
